Senior Citizen Saving scheme (SCSS)

Introduction: Senior Citizens Savings Scheme (SCSS) is a government-backed savings instrument offered to Indian residents aged 60 years and over. The deposit matures after 5 years from the date of account opening but can be extended once by an additional 3 years.
Eligibility: An Individual who has attained 60 years of age can open an account with the post office or with certain Public/Private sector Banks. He/she can open the account either individually or jointly with spouse. If a person is retired on superannuation or otherwise, he is eligible to open an account at the age of 55 years onwards. Defence personnel can open an account from age 50 years onwards.
Interest Rates: As of this writing, the interest rate is 7.4 percent. The rate of interest is reviewed quarterly by the Ministry of Finance and hence is subject to change. This interest is calculated & credited quarterly.
Taxability: Investments made in SCSS account are eligible for income tax deduction up to Rs.1.5 lakhs under section 80C of the Income Tax Act. The Interest credited is fully taxable. In case the interest amount is greater than 50,000, Tax deducted at source (TDS) provisions would apply.
Deposit limits: Deposits can be made in sum with a minimum deposit of Rs 1000 & in multiples of Rs. 1000 thereafter. The maximum SCSS limit deposit is Rs.15 lakhs.
